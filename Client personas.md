# Client Personas — Bob's Mutual Funds Demo

## Alex Johnson (demo-client-001)

Alex Johnson is a 38-year-old male marketing manager living in Austin, TX, earning approximately $95,000 per year. He has been a Bob's Mutual Funds customer for several years and holds a mix of a Roth IRA and a joint taxable account with his spouse, with a combined portfolio value of roughly $241,570. Alex is in the wealth-accumulation stage of life — his primary goal is growing retirement savings while maintaining some liquidity for near-term goals like a home renovation. He is comfortable with technology and generally self-sufficient, but occasionally calls in when something looks confusing on his statement or when he wants a second opinion before making a fund switch.

**Demo intents:**
1. **Roth conversion strategy** — Alex wants to understand whether it makes sense to convert a portion of his Traditional IRA to a Roth IRA this year given his current tax bracket.
2. **Taxable account rebalancing** — Alex noticed his taxable account has drifted heavily toward tech stocks and wants to discuss rebalancing and potential tax-loss harvesting opportunities.
3. **Increase Roth IRA contribution** — Alex wants to set up automatic monthly contributions to his Roth IRA and confirm he hasn't exceeded the annual contribution limit.
4. **Retirement timeline check** — Alex is 38 and wants a gut-check on whether his current savings rate and allocation put him on track for retirement at 65.

## Maria Chen (demo-client-002)

Maria Chen is a 67-year-old female retired public school teacher living in Scottsdale, AZ, receiving a pension plus Social Security and drawing from her Traditional IRA. Her portfolio totals approximately $890,000 spread across a Traditional IRA, a Roth IRA, and a taxable account. Maria's primary concerns are managing Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs), minimizing taxes in retirement, and ensuring her beneficiary designations are current for her two adult children. She is cautious with technology and prefers to speak with a live agent rather than self-serve; she values patient, jargon-free explanations and consistency with the same representative.

**Demo intents:**
1. **Required Minimum Distribution calculation** — Maria turned 73 this year and needs help calculating her first RMD from her Traditional IRA — she wants to know the exact amount and the deadline to avoid the penalty.
2. **Move to capital preservation during volatility** — Maria is concerned about recent market swings and wants to shift a meaningful portion of her IRA into a stable-value or money market fund temporarily.
3. **Update beneficiary designations** — Maria recently remarried and wants to update the beneficiary on both her IRA and taxable account to reflect her new spouse and revise the contingent beneficiaries.
4. **Systematic withdrawal planning** — Maria is fully retired and wants to set up a monthly withdrawal from her taxable account to cover living expenses while leaving her IRA to grow as long as possible.

## Jordan Williams (demo-client-003)

Jordan Williams is a 26-year-old non-binary registered nurse living in Chicago, IL, earning approximately $68,000 per year. They opened their first investment account — a Roth IRA — just 18 months ago and are still learning the basics of investing. Their total portfolio is modest at around $23,300, consisting of the Roth IRA and a small individual taxable account they started after a bonus. Jordan's goal is to build long-term wealth and get into the habit of consistent investing through an automated plan. They have lots of questions about contribution limits, fund selection, and whether they are "doing it right," and they appreciate encouragement alongside factual guidance.

**Demo intents:**
1. **First-time fund selection** — Jordan just opened their Roth IRA six months ago and left it in the default money market — they want guidance on which funds to invest in and how to think about risk at their age.
2. **Set up automatic contributions** — Jordan wants to automate a $200/month contribution to their Roth IRA so they can hit the annual limit over time without having to think about it.
3. **Emergency fund vs. investing** — Jordan has $4,800 in their taxable account and is unsure whether to keep it as an emergency fund or invest it — they want help thinking through the tradeoff.
4. **Understanding account types** — Jordan's new employer offers a 401(k) and Jordan wants to understand how it fits alongside their existing Roth IRA and whether they should prioritize one over the other.

## Robert Martinez (demo-client-004)

Robert Martinez is a 51-year-old male small business owner (plumbing contractor) based in Denver, CO, with variable annual income averaging around $180,000. He holds a SEP-IRA with a current balance of approximately $445,000, plus an individual taxable account. Robert is focused on maximizing tax-deferred contributions each year, understanding how his SEP-IRA contributions interact with his business income, and beginning to think about a succession or retirement timeline in the next 10–15 years. He is direct, time-pressed, and values concise answers; he has little patience for being transferred multiple times and expects the agent to already have his account context when the call begins.

**Demo intents:**
1. **Maximize SEP-IRA contribution** — Robert had a strong revenue year and wants to confirm the maximum SEP-IRA contribution he can make before the tax filing deadline and how to initiate the deposit.
2. **Roth conversion in a down year** — Robert's business income is lower than usual this year and he wants to explore converting a portion of his SEP-IRA to Roth to take advantage of the lower tax bracket.
3. **Taxable account concentration concern** — Robert realizes that about 60% of his taxable account is in a single sector and wants to discuss a gradual diversification plan that minimizes the tax hit from selling.
4. **Compare SEP-IRA vs. Solo 401(k)** — Robert is considering switching from a SEP-IRA to a Solo 401(k) for next year to allow for Roth contributions and higher limits — he wants a side-by-side comparison.
